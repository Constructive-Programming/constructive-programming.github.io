Consulting

I help teams make their codebases correct by construction.

I'm Rodolfo Hansen — author of cats-eo, designer of the Fixed language, and the writer behind a running series on type-driven design at rodolfohansen.medium.com — on total program cardinality, the cost dynamic types accumulate as a codebase grows, dependent and refinement types in mainstream languages, the case for using more optics, and where functional architecture stops earning its complexity.

I work primarily in Scala and Rust. The thread that runs through every codebase I touch is narrowing: shrinking the set of values a type can hold so that fewer wrong programs compile. That instinct shapes how I review code, model data, and move teams toward typed functional idioms without rewriting the world.

Engagements I take on:

  • IC contributions — ship features in Scala or Rust alongside your existing engineering team.
  • Architecture and advisory — review designs through a cardinality lens, narrow type signatures, identify where capability-based abstractions, refinement types, or algebraic effects unlock simpler and safer code.
  • Optics, effects and recursion deep dives — bring cats-eo, Monocle, droste, or your effect system of choice to bear on knotty data-pipeline or state-management problems.
  • Workshops and training — adapted from the constructive programming workshops and the Medium series, for your team's stack and seniority.

Engagements range from a focused code review to a multi-month embedded role. If any of this sounds useful, I'd love to hear from you.
